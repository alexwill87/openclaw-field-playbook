## 2. REF-01 — The OpenClaw Playbook (Steinberg, LeanPub)

**URL:** https://leanpub.com/theopenclawplaybook  
**Author:** Dennis Steinberg  
**Status:** Work in progress (Parts I and II complete, Parts III and IV announced)  
**Price:** $22.99 suggested / $16.99 minimum  
**Pages:** 139 (current)  
**Format:** PDF, EPUB  
**Free sample:** Available at leanpub.com

### Tagline
*"A Prompt-First Guide to Making Your Agent Useful"*

### Summary
Personal productivity book. Teaches the reader to configure OpenClaw around their life — identity, memory, routines, relationships, weekly rhythm. No code. Prompt-only. Four personas (Alex, Sam, Mira, Jordan) walk through each chapter. Claims the system works with any agent platform, not just OpenClaw.

### Table of contents

**Introduction: The Spiral**

**Part I: Understand** (chapters 1-6) — *complete*
1. Who Are You? — Defining Your Identity
2. Memory — How Your Agent Remembers
3. Giving Your Agent Eyes — Connecting Data Sources
4. Security & Trust — Defining the Relationship
5. Your Task System — The Bridge Between Thinking and Doing
6. The Morning Briefing — Your First Daily Win

**Part II: Automate** (chapters 7-11) — *complete*
7. Recognizing and Taking Over Routines
8. Filtering Information — Signal from Noise
9. Reminders & Relationships — People, Not Just Tasks
10. The Weekly Rhythm
11. Reducing Options — One Recommendation, One Alternative

**Part III: Decide** (chapters 12-18) — *in progress*
12. From Reaction to Proaction — Reading Signals
13. Scoring — Giving Your Agent Judgment
14. Nudging — Gentle Pushes in the Right Direction
15. Seasonal Intelligence — Your Year Has Patterns Too
16. The Feedback Loop — Calibrating Together
17. Unlocking Autonomy — Trust Grows Gradually
18. Your Agent Monitors Itself

**Part IV: Reflect** (chapters 19-24) — *announced*
19. The Journal — Reflection with Context
20. Recognizing Patterns — Your Agent as Analyst
21. Self-Experiments — Testing What Actually Works For You
22. The Long View — Time Capsules & Changelog
23. Trigger-Action Programming — Making It Truly Yours
24. Growth Partner — The Agent Didn't Grow. You Did.

### Key concepts
- "Memory zones" architecture (hot/warm/cold)
- Three-level scoring system (most signals cost zero tokens)
- Weekly rhythm with preview + review anchors
- Personas as a teaching device

### Strengths
- Extremely personal angle — rare vulnerability ("the book was written with the system it teaches")
- Structured progression: understand → automate → decide → reflect
- Honest about work in progress status

### Gaps relative to our playbook
- No entrepreneurship angle — purely personal productivity
- No business infrastructure (installation, sovereignty, security)
- No French context / multilingual support
- Not open source / not community-contributed
- Not designed to be ingested by an agent

## 5. REF-04 — openclaw-playbook (meircohen, GitHub)

**URL:** https://github.com/meircohen/openclaw-playbook  
**Author:** Meir Cohen (@MeirCohen on X — "12 patents, zero lines of code")  
**License:** MIT  
**Stars:** 6 (early stage)  
**Format:** GitHub repo with Markdown files

### Summary
Practical config repo. Not a guide to read — a template to clone. Core files: SOUL.md (agent personality), AGENTS.md (operating manual), USER.md (who you are), MEMORY.md (long-term memory). Claims 20% better accuracy and 70% lower token costs with skill graphs vs flat files.

### File structure
```
SOUL.md          — agent personality + communication rules
AGENTS.md        — operating system: startup, memory, safety, autonomy
USER.md          — template for telling agent who you are
MEMORY.md        — long-term memory template
memory/          — memory system architecture
skill-graphs/    — structured knowledge system (benchmarked)
```

### Benchmark results (self-reported)
- Flat files: 39/50 accuracy, ~12K tokens/session
- Skill graphs v2: 47/50 accuracy, ~3.5K tokens/session

### Strengths
- Clone-and-use immediately — no reading required
- Benchmark data adds credibility
- MIT license — fully reusable
- Personal credibility ("12 patents, zero lines of code")

### Gaps relative to our playbook
- No community contribution model
- No entrepreneurship context
- Minimal documentation
- Not maintained (2 commits only)
- No multilingual support

## 10. Positioning implications for our playbook

### What the market is missing

1. **A complete guide from installation to maintenance** — nothing covers the full journey
2. **Entrepreneurship framing without hype** — REF-02 uses "earn while you sleep" language that erodes trust. The market needs honest field reports
3. **Open source + community model** — REF-04 is the only open source reference, but it's minimal
4. **French context** — zero French-adapted resources exist
5. **Agent-ingestion architecture** — no existing guide is designed to be read by an agent to bootstrap its own setup
6. **Sovereignty as a first principle** — NVIDIA covers it technically, nobody makes it a business philosophy

### Naming landscape

"Playbook" is used by at least 4 existing resources. This is a signal of market convergence around the term — and a risk of confusion. Our differentiator must be in the subtitle and the content, not the name alone.

**Title risk:** `theopenclawplaybook.com` is already taken by someone else.  
**Mitigation:** `openclawfieldplaybook.com` (our target) is a different domain and clearly ours.

## 11. What we do that no one else does

Three things that are genuinely unique in this landscape:

**1. Clone-and-deploy for entrepreneurs, not just technically**
REF-04 (meircohen) is clone-and-deploy for technical users. Nobody does it for the full business journey — installation through maintenance — for non-developers.

**2. Designed to be ingested by an AI agent**
No existing resource has structured metadata, agent instructions, and parsing-ready content. This is new. It makes the playbook recursive — it can configure the agent that will use it.

**3. Community-maintained, open source, with AI moderation**
No paid book can match a community that improves daily. The asymmetry grows over time.
