Chapter 1 -- Definition

What is OpenClaw, concretely? What does it do, what doesn't it do, and why does it deserve your time?

This chapter lays the foundations. It defines what OpenClaw is, what sets it apart from other AI tools, and why concepts like proactivity and data sovereignty matter to an entrepreneur. It ends with the mental architecture you need before moving on to installation.

Table of Contents

  • 1.1 -- What OpenClaw is (and isn't) — The fundamental mental model: agent framework, composability, and what changes compared to a chatbot.

  • 1.2 -- The distinction between proactive and reactive — The Steinberg spiral and the four levels that separate a passive assistant from a system that moves forward on its own.

  • 1.3 -- OpenClaw vs other AI assistants — Honest comparison with ChatGPT, Copilot, AutoGPT and CrewAI: who does what, and where OpenClaw fits.

  • 1.4 -- Why sovereignty matters for an entrepreneur — Data, GDPR, infrastructure control: why it's not just a marketing argument.

  • 1.5 -- The mental architecture before you start — The 3 layers (identity, visibility, action) and the concept of universal and business-specific pillars.

  • 1.6 -- What you're going to build in this guide — Overview of the target architecture and what you'll have at the end of the journey.

Entrepreneur's Path

Are you an entrepreneur, craftsperson or small business owner? These sections are written for you, without technical jargon.

  • 1.7 -- What does a day with an AI agent look like? — A typical day, hour by hour. What changes concretely.

  • 1.8 -- How much does it really cost? — The real numbers: 10-25 EUR/month, no surprises, no commitment.

  • 1.9 -- Is this complicated for me? — Two paths: have it installed or follow the guide. Both are accessible.

  • 1.10 -- What happens if I do nothing? — What inaction costs in time, prospects, and energy.

  • 1.11 -- How do I get started? — The concrete steps, in order, to start this week.
